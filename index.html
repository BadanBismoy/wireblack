The problem

Banking was built for predictable salaries. Today's earners don't work that way.

Traditional institutions were designed around physical branches and standardized paychecks. The modern economy runs on freelancers, creators, migrant workers, students and digital entrepreneurs — and financial institutions still operate on outdated assumptions.

01

Outdated banking systems

Generic banking and insurance products for every kind of community and occupation, with no distinction between them.

02

Fragmented services

Customers can't access the full range of financial services in one place, and digital experiences remain poor.

03

Slow onboarding

Rigid underwriting systems and little to no personalization slow customers down at the exact moment they need speed.

04

Exclusion by default

High remittance fees and no access to formal credit scoring shut out anyone without a traditional income record.

Freelancers & migrant workers

  • Irregular income and currency conversion issues
  • No formal credit history
  • Difficulty accessing loans or credit
  • Missing corporate benefits and safety nets

Students & Gen Z

  • Low engagement with slow, branch-reliant banking
  • Poor savings habits, complex documentation
  • Limited credit access with no formal history
  • No access to multi-currency cards

Online businesses

  • Slow digital transformation, manual processes
  • Limited access to short-term financing
  • High collateral requirements
  • Cash-flow instability and inventory risk
Why current solutions fail

Neobanks digitized the branch. They didn't rethink who banking is for.

1

Banking isn't behavior-driven

Banks rarely consider real financial behavior — spending patterns, cash-flow consistency or digital reputation — when deciding who to serve.

Impact: personalized guidance is almost nonexistent
2

One-size-fits-all products

A university student, a freelance designer, a migrant worker and a small business owner receive nearly identical products despite completely different needs.

Impact: low relevance, poor engagement
3

Documentation creates exclusion

Many digital workers and underserved communities can't meet paperwork, formal employment or collateral requirements — even with stable income.

Impact: millions remain excluded from formal finance

Tailored products

What each community actually gets.

Every product below solves one concrete cash-flow problem for that community, with a specific mechanic — not a generic feature.

Income stabilizer

Income Stabilizer

A time-triggered safety net for the silence between clients.

Mechanic

Builds a 3-month ring-fenced retainer that unlocks only once the platform detects a 30+ day pipeline collapse.

FX protection

USD Earning Stability Lock

Protects against currency volatility eroding purchasing power.

Mechanic

Locks earnings in USD at a growth rate, auto-converting only when the home-currency exchange rate is optimal.

Financing

Invoice Financing

Solves immediate cash-flow gaps between finishing work and getting paid.

Mechanic

Borrow up to 80% against a verified but unpaid invoice — no fixed EMI, repays automatically when the invoice clears.

Insurance

Coverage for job loss abroad, deportation, unpaid salaries and employer abuse.

Insight

Credit

Instant home-country credit disbursed to the family in minutes.

Underwriting

Wealth building

A personal wealth builder, kept separate from family remittances.

Mechanic

Gamified savings

Mechanic

Inflation hedge
